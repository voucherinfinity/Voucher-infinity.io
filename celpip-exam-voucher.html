Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program (CELPIP)

January 1, 2021 by Mark

CELPIP is an acronym for the Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program, which is recognized by the IRCC (Immigration, Refugee & Citizenship Canada) as an English Language Proficiency test for individuals applying for permanent residency and citizenship in Canada. It’s important to note that CELPIP is not accepted for SDS (Student Direct Stream) applications. The CELPIP exam is a computer-based test that includes four sections: Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing. CELPIP Exam Eligibility

There are no eligibility requirements for the CELPIP test; all you need is a valid passport for registration. It is highly advisable to be well-prepared for the exam before proceeding with the registration process.

Types of CELPIP Exam

There are two types of CELPIP tests, CELPIP General and CELPIP General LS. The CELPIP General is recognized for University Admission and Permanent Residency applications globally, with a test duration of 3 hours covering four modules that are Reading, Listening, Writing and Speaking. Moreover, it is available worldwide.

On the other hand, the CELPIP General LS is accepted solely for citizenship applications and is limited to Canada. It has a shorter duration of 1 hour 10 minutes, focusing on two modules: Listening and Speaking. These two CELPIP tests provide different purposes and are distinguished by their scope and availability.

CELPIP Exam Pattern

The exam is divided into four sections: reading, writing, listening, and speaking with the test duration of 3 hours:

  1. For the listening section, there are 38 questions spread across six parts, with an allotted time of 47-55 minutes:
    • Part 1: Listening to Problem-Solving (8 questions)
    • Part 2: Listening to a Daily Life Conversation (5 questions)
    • Part 3: Listening for Information (6 questions)
    • Part 4: Listening to a News Item (5 questions)
    • Part 5: Listening to a Discussion (8 questions)
    • Part 6: Listening to Viewpoints (6 questions)
  2. For the reading section, there are four parts with a total of 38 questions, to be completed within 55-60 minutes
    • Part 1: Reading Correspondence (11 questions)
    • Part 2: Reading to Apply a Diagram (8 questions)
    • Part 3: Reading for Information (9 questions)
    • Part 4: Reading for Viewpoints (10 questions)
  3. The writing section consists of two tasks and takes 53-60 minutes:
    • Task 1: Writing an Email
    • Task 2: Responding to Survey Questions
  4. The speaking section comprises eight tasks, and candidates have 15-20 minutes to complete them:
    • Task 1:Giving Advice
    • Task 2:Talking about a Personal Experience
    • Task 3:Describing a Scene
    • Task 4:Making Predictions
    • Task 5: Comparing and Persuading
    • Task 6:Dealing with a Difficult Situation
    • Task 7:Expressing Opinions
    • Task 8:Describing an Unusual Situation

CELPIP Exam Registration

To register for the CELPIP General Test in India, you must complete the online registration process on the CELPIP website and make the necessary payment. The fee for the CELPIP General test in India is Rs.14,750, inclusive of GST. If you wish to obtain a hard copy of your score report, you can request it by paying additional charges, and the scorecard will be sent to your provided address. Payment for the examination fee can be made through various online methods, such as credit cards, debit cards, net banking, Paytm (wallet), and UPI. It’s worth noting that there is no need to worry about international activation, as the payment will be deducted in INR.

About CELPIP Exam Centers

There are 8 CELPIP General test centers available in India, and candidates from India have the flexibility to select any center of their choice. On the exam day, it is essential to obtain the original passport and the test confirmation for the check-in process. Each test center may have its own set of rules and regulations, but it is recommended that candidates arrive at the test center 45 minutes before the scheduled start of the test.

CELPIP Result & Scores

Your test results are typically available 4-5 working days after the test date. You will receive an email and SMS notification once the results are available in your CELPIP account. If you prefer, you can also request a hard copy of the results, with additional charges depending on your location. The scores both overall and for each module are given on a scale of 12 points.

Authorization of CELPIP Exam

The CELPIP General exams are authorized for various immigration programs and authorities, including:

  • Permanent Resident Status
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
  • Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
  • Start-up Visa Program
  • Various Provincial Nominee Programs
  • Universities and colleges
  • Professional Designations

    • Validity of the CELPIP Test Scores
