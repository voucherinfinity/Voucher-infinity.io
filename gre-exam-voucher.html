GRE Exam Eligibility

There are no specific eligibility criteria for GRE as determined by ETS (Educational Testing Service). Anyone can apply for GRE exams there is no age limit and any qualified person can appear on the GRE exam. Although, those candidates applying for master’s they need to give the GRE test first. Before registering for the GRE exam candidates have to keep in mind that they are required to produce valid and original passports for further process as proof of identity in the exam center. Important: From July 1, 2021 Candidates who appear for GRE exams can use their aadhar card as a part of their identity proof during the registration process.

GRE Exam Types

There are two types of GRE Exam;

  • GRE General Test
  • GRE Subject Test

    • GRE General Test

    ETS mentioned that GRE General Test is a standardized test to calculate the candidate’s overall academic readiness who wants to attend graduate universities. Through the general test, GRE evaluates the candidate’s analytical writing, verbal reasoning skills and quantitative ability. Applicants can apply for GRE general test according to their convenience around the year.

    GRE Subject Test

    GRE General Test evaluates the applicant’s ability on a peculiar subject. In the GRE Subject Test, applicants should note that this test only extends to chemistry, mathematics, physics and psychology. Particularly GRE Subject Test is essential for getting admission to specialized courses. It only happens three times a year only in a paper-based way. This only occurs in September, October and April.

    GRE Exam Pattern

    The GRE exam pattern is divided into three sections Analytical Writing, Verbal Reasoning and Quantitative Reasoning. The analytical writing section always appears in the first place however the other sections like verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning and unscored section appear in any order. Furthermore, patterns may also differ for online exams and paper-based exams.

    Sections in GRE Exam Pattern

    Analytic Writing – (1 section – two tasks) (60 minutes)
    Verbal Reasoning – (2 sections – 40 questions) (60 minutes)
    Quantitative Reasoning – (2 sections – 40 questions) (70 minutes)

    GRE Exam Syllabus

    The GRE exam syllabus is different for both GRE exams whether it is GRE general test or the GRE subject test. GRE general test is a standard exam that tests a candidate’s skills in analytical writing, verbal reasoning and quantitative reasoning. Candidates who want to specialize in a particular subject, they need to take a GRE subject test that is required by the university at the time of admission. GRE Subject Test basically makes you technical expertise in a particular field.
