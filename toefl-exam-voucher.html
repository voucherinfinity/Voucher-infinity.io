Types of TOEFL Exam

It's essential for candidates to understand that the ETS TOEFL examination offers three distinct formats: the TOEFL iBT (Internet-based Test), the TOEFL Paper-based Test, and the TOEFL Home Edition, catering to diverse preferences and circumstances of test-takers. The TOEFL iBT stands out as the favored examination format, with an overwhelming majority of over 98% of TOEFL test-takers worldwide opting for this internet-based medium, reflecting its widespread popularity and recognition among language proficiency assessments.

TOEFL Exam Eligibility

The TOEFL exam is typically undertaken by students who aspire to pursue their academic endeavors abroad, reflecting a common pathway for individuals aiming to access international educational opportunities. Many prospective test-takers diligently research the TOEFL eligibility criteria as they embark on their exam preparation journey, underscoring the importance of understanding the requirements before diving into their study efforts. TOEFL Exam Pattern

The structure of the TOEFL exam is delineated into four distinct sections, namely, Reading, Listening, Speaking, and Writing are the core components of the TOEFL exam, each contributing to the overall score that evaluates candidates' English language proficiency comprehensively. Engaging in practice with TOEFL sample papers proves instrumental in enhancing your performance and ultimately achieving a commendable score on the examination. In each section, candidates can attain scores ranging from 0 to 30, culminating in a comprehensive total score that spans from 0 to 120, thus offering a detailed evaluation of their English language proficiency across various skill domains. Candidates can expect access to their TOEFL scores online approximately six days after the examination date. Following the release, ETS facilitates the transmission of up to four official score reports directly to the educational institutions designated by the student, ensuring a seamless and efficient delivery of their assessment results.

TOEFL Exam Scores

TOEFL Results: After taking the TOEFL exam, candidates need not wait much for their results. Students can view their unofficial reading and listening scores at the end of the exam. When the official TOEFL scores are issued online, candidates will receive an email about it. To access your TOEFL iBT scores, simply log into your account on the designated platform. Typically, scores become available to candidates approximately six days following the test date. For individuals who opted for the TOEFL Home Edition, results are typically accessible within 6-10 days from the exam date. The paper copy of the score the candidate has requested will be mailed within 10 days after the exam date. The delivery time is based on local postal services.

TOEFL score validity

The TOEFL score is valid for two years from the date of declaration of the result. If the candidates are not satisfied with their scores, they can try again as there is no limitation on retaking the exam. Though, there should be a minimum three-day gap before candidates retake their exams.
